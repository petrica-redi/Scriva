## 1. Inaccuracies or overclaims to fix

| Location | Issue | Suggested change |
|----------|--------|-------------------|
| **CV — "Supervised ~100 staff across 7 countries"** | "Supervised" can imply direct line management of 100 people. If you coordinated governance for 7 projects that collectively involved ~100 staff, a reviewer may question the wording. | Use: **"Coordinated governance for 7 health projects involving ~100 staff across 7 countries"** (or "Coordinated … with project teams totaling ~100 staff") so it’s clear you’re not claiming 100 direct reports. |
| **CV — "Conducted investment impact analysis triggering $77.8M follow-on funding"** | "Triggering" suggests your analysis alone caused the decision. Funders use many inputs. | Use: **"Conducted investment impact analysis that informed a $77.8M follow-on funding decision (Indonesia)"** — keeps the impact without overclaiming causation. |
| **Cover letter — "OECD-adjacent institutions"** | ToR partners are UNICEF, OECD, World Bank, Global Fund. "OECD-adjacent" is vague and could be read as overclaiming OECD experience. | Prefer: **"working relationships with World Bank, UNICEF, and multilateral health financing partners"** (drop "OECD-adjacent" unless you have direct OECD work), or: **"experience with institutions that partner with OECD on health and financing"** if you have a concrete example. |

## 2. Precision (taxonomy vs. framework vs. domain)

| Location | Point | Suggested tweak |
|----------|--------|------------------|
| **CV & CL — ROInnovate as "investment taxonomy"** | ROInnovate is an **innovation/R&D** funding taxonomy (government → startups/enterprises), not a **digital health** or **health** investment taxonomy. The *skill* (designing classification for investment flows) is what matters; the *domain* differs. | Add one short phrase so a reviewer doesn’t think you claimed a prior digital health taxonomy: e.g. **"Designed investment taxonomy and financial architecture for national innovation agency (ROInnovate, €20M) — innovation funding flows; methodology directly applicable to digital health investment classification."** |
| **CV & CL — Finland "digital health maturity"** | You tested a **maturity/interoperability framework**, not an **investment** taxonomy. The *method* (national testing, stakeholder engagement, recommendations) is what’s analogous. | Clarify: **"Assessed digital health maturity and system integration at national level (Finland) — testing a framework against national realities; methodology directly applicable to testing the investment taxonomy in country."** So "framework testing" and "methodology" are aligned to the ToR without overstating content match. |
