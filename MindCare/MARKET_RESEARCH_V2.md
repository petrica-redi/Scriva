## SECTION 2: Romanian Market Landscape

### 2.1 Market Size & Structure

**Psychiatrists in Romania:**
- Approximately **2,000–2,500 psychiatrists** (including residents) per Romanian College of Physicians data
- ~1,500 active practicing psychiatrists
- Concentrated in major cities: Bucharest (~400), Cluj-Napoca (~150), Iași (~120), Timișoara (~100), Brașov, Sibiu, Constanța

**Psychologists / Psychotherapists:**
- Romanian College of Psychologists registers approximately **25,000–30,000 psychologists**
- Of these, approximately **8,000–10,000 are active clinical/counseling psychologists** with psychotherapy competence
- Growing rapidly: psychotherapy has become increasingly popular post-COVID, with 20-30% growth in private practice registrations since 2020
- Estimated **3,000–5,000 active private practice psychotherapists** seeing clients regularly

**Total Addressable Market (Romania):**
- ~1,500 psychiatrists + ~5,000 active psychotherapists = **~6,500 mental health professionals**
- At €30–50/month average pricing: **TAM of €2.3M–3.9M/year** in Romania alone

### 2.3 Regulatory Environment

**GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)**
- Romania is an EU member → full GDPR applies
- Mental health data = "special category" data (Art. 9) requiring explicit consent
- Data processing requires legal basis (consent or legitimate interest)
- Data Protection Authority: ANSPDCP (Autoritatea Națională de Supraveghere a Prelucrării Datelor cu Caracter Personal)
- **Key requirement:** Data must be processable in EU or with adequate safeguards
- AI processing of health data requires Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)

**CNAS (Casa Națională de Asigurări de Sănătate)**
- Romania's national health insurance system
- Psychiatrists can work within CNAS contracts (public/semi-public)
- Psychotherapy is **not covered by CNAS** — patients pay out of pocket
- CNAS-contracted psychiatrists must use specific documentation formats
- Private practitioners have more flexibility in documentation tools

**Telemedicine Regulations:**
- Romania legalized telemedicine during COVID-19 (Emergency Ordinance 196/2020)
- Telehealth consultations are reimbursable under CNAS (with conditions)
- Patient consent required for telemedicine
- Data security requirements for telemedicine platforms

**Romanian College of Psychologists (CPR):**
- Regulates psychologists and psychotherapists
- Mandatory registration and competence certification
- Ethical code requires documentation of sessions
- No specific requirements about digital tools (flexibility for MindCare)

**Key Regulatory Implications for MindCare:**
1. EU data hosting required (or adequate safeguards) → use EU cloud infrastructure
2. Explicit patient consent for AI processing of session data
3. DPIA required before launch
4. No regulatory barriers to AI-assisted documentation specifically
5. Opportunity to become the "GDPR-compliant" alternative to US tools

### 2.4 Digital Adoption Level

- **Smartphone penetration:** ~85% in Romania (high)
- **Internet access:** ~90% in urban areas, ~65% in rural
- **Digital health adoption:** Low-moderate. Romanians use health apps for fitness but rarely for clinical care
- **Clinician tech comfort:**
  - Under 40: High comfort with digital tools, early adopters
  - 40–55: Moderate, willing if clear ROI
  - Over 55: Low, resistant to change
- **COVID accelerator:** Telehealth adoption jumped from ~10% to ~40% of private practitioners. Many retained digital tools post-pandemic.
- **Key barrier:** Trust in AI for clinical decisions is low. Education needed.

**Recommended Focus Areas (Top 3):**

1. **Depression & Anxiety** (combined) — Highest volume. Every therapist and psychiatrist sees these. Universal appeal for the platform. Documentation is repetitive but important (treatment response tracking, PHQ-9/GAD-7 scores, medication adjustments). AI adds clear value in tracking longitudinal progress.

3. **Trauma/PTSD** — Complex documentation needs. Sessions are narrative-heavy (perfect for AI scribe). Treatment modalities like EMDR have specific documentation requirements. Growing awareness post-pandemic and due to Ukrainian refugee influx in Romania.

**Recommended Entry Strategy:**

**Phase 1 (Months 0–12): Solo private practitioners + telehealth-only practices**
- Fastest decision-making (no committees, no procurement)
- Highest documentation pain (no admin support)
- Self-service onboarding possible
- Word-of-mouth in tight professional communities
- Romanian psychotherapist community is close-knit → viral potential

**Phase 2 (Months 6–18): Small group practices (2–10 clinicians)**
- One champion adopter can bring in the whole practice
- Higher ARPU (average revenue per user)
- Standardization need = strong value proposition
- Practice managers become advocates
